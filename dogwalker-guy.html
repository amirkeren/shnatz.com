Dogwalker Guy

The goal of this project is to create or iterate upon a responsive flow for a local business. After choosing a business and discussing with the client, I chose to update the business's existing brand, UX and UI.

Background

Dogwalker Guy is a small business offering dog-walking services and dog day-care in Tel Aviv, Israel. Guy is the owner and main dog-walker. In his current website you can find information about the business, prices, testimonials and contact methods. He would love to update his online appearance, and potentially increase his exposure to new clients and bookings.

Guy currently has 15 steady clients, and his goal is to expand his audience to have about 30 by the end of 2024.

To define the main goals for the design, I needed to learn about the current pain points and needs, both for the client and for the users.

Research

Client Interview

As a first step of my research, I interviewed the client (Guy) to better understand his needs and goals. I asked him questions about his target audience, main goals for the website, pain points and suggestions.

Secondary Research

Some dog walkers use apps to schedule walks and track routes, ensuring the well-being and exercise needs of each dog are met. Some offer additional services such as dog day-care and dog-sitting. However, most dog-walkers don't use a website to promote themselves and receive new clients through word of mouth.

Usability Testing

To evaluate the current website's UX/UI intuitiveness and experience, I have conducted user experience testing. In order to test the current user behavior, I took screenshots of the existing website and created a prototype on Figma. Then I created the tests using Lyssna's interface, syncing it with Figma.

Participants were asked to complete two small tests:

  • Contact test (task-specific) - to test how easily users can contact Guy.
  • General experience test - asking users to browse freely and give feedback.

Main Takeaways and Next Steps

Synthesizing all parts of my research, these were my conclusions for the next steps:

  • Add an "about" page
  • Make it easy for users to share the page
  • Replan navigation so it's more intuitive, including adding a footer (many participants commented that the website is not intuitive).
  • Have clearer ways to contact the business
  • CTAs should be more engaging
  • Add an online booking option

Key Takeaways

This project involved reiterating on an existing dog walking small business website, focusing on creating a user-friendly and visually engaging online presence. The new design features a more intuitive navigation, clear information about services, and easy contact options for potential clients. Additionally, it offers a new online booking feature to create a seamless booking process for new and existing clients. I learned a lot about communicating with clients, understanding their pain points and creating an improved experience.
